Our History

Liverpool was a hotbed for musical talent in the 1950's, with several bands touring the city's musical venues. John formed his first group, The Quarrymen, in 1957. He was 16 at the time. Paul was in attendance at one of the Quarrymen's early performances, and was introduced to John after the show.

Paul played "Twenty Flight Rock" for John on the guitar, and his ability was so impressive that he was asked to join the group, aged just 15.

George was a huge fan of The Quarrymen, and would regularly go to hear them play. On one ocassion he was asked to stand in for an abcent guitarist. His talent led to him join the group in 1958.

In 1960, the band changed their name to 'The Beatles', with John, Paul, and George all founding members.

Having tried a number of different drummers, Ringo eventually joined The Beatles in 1962. The rest is history.
